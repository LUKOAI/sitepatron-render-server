Every additional sale on Amazon
costs more and more

Standard methods for boosting sales all come with their own costs. Every single one. Each in its own way.

✗
Amazon Ads (CPC + ACOS). You pay for every click. ACOS outside of top bestsellers is typically 15-25% — a slice of your margin eaten by ads. Conversion is usually 4-5% (sometimes up to 10%, but rarely).
✗
Influencers and affiliates. You pay commission on every sale. With 200-1,000 orders per month, even 5% adds up to real money. And it's often an episodic channel — one post and it's over.
✗
Traditional SEO agencies. $500-1,500/month, results only in months 6-12. They require long contracts and continuous cooperation. You can't just start and stop when you want.
✗
Display ads / banners. CPM is high, conversion is low. People ignore banners. Brand awareness barely grows — sales even less.
✗
Your own brand site. Requires hiring copywriters, developers, SEO and AI-search specialists. Domain, hosting, maintenance. First results after a year.

How Amazon works on our Site

Briefly on why displaying Amazon products on our Site is legal — and what it means for you as a seller.

📋 Displaying Amazon products — legal basis

The display of products from Amazon on our Site — their photos, prices, titles and descriptions — is governed by my affiliate agreement with Amazon (Amazon EU Associates Programme IP Licence). This agreement grants me the right to legally present products from the Amazon platform in the editorial Content of my Site.

What this means in practice: Amazon grants me a license to display products. A seller (you, your competitor, anyone) cannot block the fact that I display some products from the platform. This is between Amazon and me — you are not a party to it.

What this gives you as a Patron: Confidence that the Site operates on a strong legal basis. The exclusivity of products within the Scope is an additional service that you purchase — it is we who undertake not to display competition during your subscription (clause 14.1 of the contract). Without your subscription — the multi-brand version stays, is legal, works.

Clauses to check in the contract: 5 (Nature of the Site), 7 (Independence from Amazon affiliation), 16 (Amazon Content and compliance).
